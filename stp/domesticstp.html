What is Bio-Pure Septic Tank ?

An RCC (Reinforced Cement Concrete) bio-Pure septic tank treats sewage and wastewater through a combination of physical and biological processes. Wastewater enters the tank through an inlet chamber, where heavy solids settle. Inside the tank, anaerobic bacteria break down organic matter without oxygen, reducing sludge volume and converting waste into gases like methane and carbon dioxide. Solids settle at the bottom as sludge, and lighter materials form scum, while the treated liquid, or effluent, moves to the next chamber. Some systems have a secondary chamber for further treatment. The effluent exits through an outlet chamber with a filter, trapping any remaining particles. Finally, the treated effluent is discharged into the soil through a soak pit or leach field, or reused for irrigation, depending on the system design and local regulations. This process significantly reduces pathogens and pollutants, making the water safe for disposal or reuse. Treated water can be used for gardening or groundwater recharge.
